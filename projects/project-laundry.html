Project Summary

From concept to profitability in three years. This project showcases the successful launch and operation of a self-service laundry. As co-founder, I blended business strategy with hands-on engineering to build a lean, low-cost operational model that maximized uptime and achieved a rapid return on investment.

From Concept to ROI

Co-founded a self-service laundry facility to serve a university campus. Directed the entire lifecycle from securing tenancy contracts and vendor negotiation to facility design. Through strict financial management and strategic pricing, the business achieved full loan repayment within 3 years.

Hands-On Maintenance & Cost Reduction

Applied engineering principles to operations. Instead of relying on expensive external contractors, I personally handled machine diagnostics and repairs. This hands-on approach, combined with rigorous preventive maintenance SOPs, slashed operational costs by 50% and maximized machine uptime.

Process Automation & Leadership

Streamlined financial operations using QuickBooks, reducing administrative overhead. Built a self-sufficient operational model by hiring and training on-site students, creating a low-cost, high-response workforce that required minimal supervision.
